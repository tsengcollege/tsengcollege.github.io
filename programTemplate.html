About This Program

Based on a curriculum that addresses the behavioral theory and its real-world application, this two-year program prepares you to step into top positions in the field. Students will receive on-the-ground training in clinical settings arranged by CSUN, with faculty guidance each step of the way. You will learn how to conduct thorough behavioral assessments, implement and monitor evidence-based interventions, and consult with parents, caregivers, and school personnel while adhering to the profession's ethical guidelines. Master of Science in Applied Behavior Analysis Program at CSUN

CSUN's Master of Science in Applied Behavior Analysis will provide you with a solid foundation for practice as a skilled, professional BCBA and/or pursue doctoral programs in Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) or related fields. The Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB®) approved coursework is embedded in the two-year cohort program so that graduates may earn their master's degrees simultaneous with becoming eligible to sit for the BCBA Examination.

What do BCBAs do?

Behavior analysts (BCBAs) focus on the direct behavioral outcomes of their interventions. They typically start by identifying current contexts that are responsible for behavioral problems, then design changes to those same contexts to promote better behavior. In doing so, the BCBA works with the people who play an active role in the context of the behavior, such as family, teachers, other caregivers, and other health care providers. Behavior analysts typically work directly with the client and caregivers to arrange practice opportunities to promote the desired behavior change.
